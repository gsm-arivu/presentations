## Chapter 3: Types of Contracts and Systems of Selection

### 3.1 Types of Contracts
* Lump Sum (Firm Fixed Price) Contract
* Time-Based (Retainer-ship) Contract
* Percentage (Success/Contingency Fee) Contract
* Indefinite Delivery Contract (Price Agreement)

---

### 3.2 Lump Sum (Firm Fixed Price) Contract
* Predefined total cost for services
* Payment linked to deliverables
* Suitable for well-defined projects
* Less flexibility for scope changes

---

### 3.3 Time-Based (Retainer-ship) Contract
* Payments based on actual time spent
* Suitable for complex, long-term projects
* Requires clear time-tracking mechanisms
* Risk of inefficiencies if not monitored properly

---

### 3.4 Percentage (Success/Contingency Fee) Contract
* Payment as a percentage of achieved results
* Suitable for financial and revenue-linked services
* Risk-based contracting with incentives
* Requires robust evaluation mechanisms

---

### 3.5 Retainer and Success (Contingency) Fee Contract
* Combination of time-based and percentage-based fees
* Initial retainer fee with performance-linked payments
* Balances financial risk and performance incentives
* Ensures consultant commitment throughout project

---

### 3.6 Indefinite Delivery Contract (Price Agreement)
* Pre-approved pricing structure for repeated services
* Flexible in terms of service frequency and quantity
* Reduces procurement lead time
* Requires strong contract management

---

### 3.7 Systems of Selection of Service Providers
* Open Competitive Bidding
* Least Cost Selection (LCS)
* Quality and Cost-Based Selection (QCBS)
* Direct Selection: Single Source Selection (SSS)
* Fixed Budget-Based Selection (FBS)

---

### 3.8 Price-Based System - Least Cost Selection (LCS)
* Selection of lowest cost technically qualified bidder
* Suitable for standard services with clear specifications
* Ensures cost efficiency but may compromise quality
* Commonly used for non-specialized services

---

### 3.9 Quality and Cost-Based Selection (QCBS)
* Balances quality and cost in selection process
* Weighted evaluation criteria (e.g., 80% quality, 20% cost)
* Encourages competition while maintaining service standards
* Suitable for complex consultancy assignments

---

### 3.10 Direct Selection: Single Source Selection (SSS)
* Directly awarding contract without competition
* Justifications required: urgency, proprietary services, lack of alternatives
* Requires approval from competent authority
* Risk of lack of competition and transparency

---

### 3.11 Fixed Budget-Based Selection (FBS)
* Fixed budget with technical evaluation criteria
* Selection based on highest technical score within budget
* Ensures best value for pre-defined budget
* Suitable for projects with strict cost limitations
